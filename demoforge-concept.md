# DemoForge — Product Concept & Vision Document

## What DemoForge Is

DemoForge turns any website or product screenshot into an interactive, guided sales demo that anyone can use — even the 52-year-old business owner who can barely send a text. No coding. No design skills. No monthly fee that makes your accountant wince.

You paste a URL or upload a photo. DemoForge captures the screen, lets you drop interactive hotspots on it, write simple guided tour steps, and spits out a shareable link. When your prospect opens that link, they walk through your product like a guided museum tour, clicking where you tell them to click, seeing what you want them to see.

This is not a screen recorder. This is not a video. This is a living, clickable, interactive clone of your product that you control completely.

## The Problem We're Solving

Every small business owner has this problem: they have a product, a service, a platform, or even just an idea — and they cannot show it to people in a way that makes sense.

- **The HVAC guy** has a customer portal but can't show it off because the real one has live customer data in it.
- **The boutique hotel** has a booking engine but the demo environment is always broken.
- **The local gym owner** wants to show the new member app but it's behind a login wall.
- **The insurance broker** has a quoting tool but it's too complex to walk someone through over the phone.
- **The landscaper** has scheduling software his crew uses, but he can't show a potential client how easy booking is.

Meanwhile, the existing "demo software" companies (Walnut, Storylane, Navattic) charge $300–$1,000 per month per seat and require a marketing team to operate them. They're built for SaaS companies with $5M in funding, not for the guy in Omaha with three employees and a dream.

## How It Works — The User Journey

### For the Demo Builder (the small business owner):

**Scenario: Mike, 54, runs a pest control company. He just launched an online scheduling portal.**

1. **Capture** — Mike opens DemoForge on his phone. He pastes the URL of his scheduling portal. DemoForge loads the page in a sandbox and takes a screenshot.
2. **Annotate** — He zooms in and drops a blue circle on the "Book Now" button. He types: "Your customers click here — they're booking in under 60 seconds."
3. **Add steps** — He drops another circle on the calendar grid. "Customers pick any open slot. We text them a confirmation instantly."
4. **Add a fake customer** — He checks a box that says "Show sample data." DemoForge fills in "Jane Smith, 123 Oak St" so the form doesn't look empty and sad.
5. **Preview** — He taps "View my demo." It plays like a guided tour: the screen dims, a spotlight follows the hotspots, pulsing rings draw attention.
6. **Share** — He taps "Copy link" and texts it to the property manager he's pitching that afternoon.

Total time: 7 minutes. Total cost: $0 for the trial, $19/month if he keeps it.

### For the Demo Viewer (the prospect):

The property manager opens the link on his phone. He sees Mike's actual portal — not a video, not a screenshot, a working replica — with a simple "Next" button at the bottom. He taps through the steps, clicks the hotspots, sees exactly what Mike wants him to see. At the end, there's a "Schedule a call with Mike" button.

He thinks: "Damn. Mike's got his shit together. I want my tenants using this."

### For the Advanced User (Tod selling to a casino):

1. **Capture the real product** — DemoForge loads WindSlot Concierge and screenshots the patron app.
2. **Replace with fake data** — Swap real patron names with "John D." and "Maria G." Swap real machines with "Lucky 7s (Simulated)."
3. **Add narrative** — "Your host gets an alert when a $5,000 player sits down. She knows their favorite drink and their last trip's comp balance."
4. **Personalize per prospect** — Before the meeting, DemoForge pulls the Angel of the Winds logo and color scheme and applies it automatically.
5. **Track engagement** — After sending the link, the dashboard shows: CEO opened it, spent 4 minutes on the "Host Alerts" screen, clicked "Learn More" twice, forwarded it to the VP of Marketing.

## The Core Features (MVP → Full Product)

### MVP (What We're Building First)

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **URL Capture** | Paste any URL, DemoForge screenshots it and builds a sandboxed clone |
| **Hotspot Dropper** | Click anywhere on the screenshot to add an interactive annotation |
| **Guided Tour** | Define a sequence of steps; viewer clicks "Next" to advance |
| **Fake Data Injection** | Toggle sample data so empty forms look populated |
| **Shareable Link** | One URL, works on any device, no login required to view |
| **Mobile-First Builder** | Entire workflow works on a phone screen |

### Phase 2 (Post-MVP)

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **Screen Recording Import** | Upload a video of your product; AI extracts frames and builds hotspots automatically |
| **AI Voiceover** | Generate a friendly guided narration from your text steps |
| **Prospect Personalization** | Auto-insert the prospect's name, logo, and colors into the demo |
| **Analytics Dashboard** | See who opened, how long they spent, where they dropped off |
| **PDF/Email Export** | Generate a one-page "demo summary" with QR code to the interactive version |
| **Multiple Demos** | Save and organize multiple demo projects under one account |

### Phase 3 (Scale)

| Feature | Description |
|---|---|
| **White-Label** | Embed DemoForge in your own domain, your own branding |
| **Team Collaboration** | Share demo projects with team members, leave comments |
| **CRM Integration** | Push demo engagement data to HubSpot, Pipedrive, etc. |
| **Template Marketplace** | Pre-built demo templates for common industries (restaurant, salon, gym, etc.) |

## Market Sizing

**TAM:** All 33 million small businesses in the US need to demo something to someone.

**SAM:** The ~5 million US small businesses that have some kind of digital product, portal, or service they need to showcase (HVAC scheduling, restaurant ordering, salon booking, real estate search, insurance quoting, etc.).

**SOM (Year 1):** 1,000 paying customers at $19–$49/mo = $300K–$600K ARR. Achievable through direct sales, local business groups (Rotary, Chamber of Commerce), and vertical SEO ("how to demo my HVAC booking system").

**Expansion:** The same technology works for internal training, onboarding, and product tutorials. A $19/mo demo tool becomes a $49/mo training platform.
